Mayung Village is a village located in the Gunung Jati sub-district, Cirebon district, West Java province. This village has an area of ​​around 113.9 hectares and a population of around 3,548 people. Mayung Village has a long and interesting history, related to the Padjadjaran kingdom and the spread of Islam in Java. According to folklore, this village originates from the name of one of King Siliwangi's mainstays, namely Ki Bahu Sasra or Ki Mayuyun, who converted to Islam after hearing the dawn call to prayer from Sunan Gunung Jati.

Mayung Village is also known as a religious and cultured village. In this village there are several places of worship and historical sites, such as the Great Mayung mosque, the tomb of Ki Bahu Sasra, the tomb of Ki Mangurun, and the tomb of Ki Singa Jaya3. Mayung Village also has quite good economic potential, especially in agriculture and fisheries. Most of the villagers work as rice, corn, soybean and vegetable farmers. In addition, there are also those who work as fishermen, traders, craftsmen, and civil servants.

Mayung Village is a beautiful and peaceful village, with beautiful natural scenery and fresh air. This village also has a friendly and harmonious community, which upholds religious and cultural values. Mayung Village is a village worth visiting and setting an example for other villages.
